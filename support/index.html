Frequently asked questions

Where is my data stored?

Lists, scores, history, language, and theme are stored in the app's local storage. Ten Note has no account, Firebase database, advertising, analytics, or tracking service, and the publisher does not receive this content.

On Android, Ten Note disables cloud backup and restore. On Android 12 and later, some device manufacturers may still transfer app files directly between devices. On iOS, an iPhone backup stored in iCloud or on your computer may include the local database. The Ten Note publisher cannot access any of these platform copies.

How do I move data to another phone?

Open Settings, enter a backup password of at least 10 characters, and create an encrypted backup. Move the .tennote file to a location you control. On the new phone, enter the same password and choose Merge backup or Replace with backup.

Backups created by an earlier version under the previous six-character minimum remain compatible and can be opened with their original password.

What if I forget the backup password?

Ten Note does not write the password to persistent storage. The password field is cleared after a backup operation or when Settings closes, so an encrypted backup cannot be recovered without the password. Create a new backup with a password you can keep safely.

How do I delete my information?

Delete items or lists individually, or use Settings → Delete all local data. Exported backup files must be deleted separately. iPhone backup copies stored in iCloud or on your computer must be managed separately through your Apple backup settings and chosen storage.

Is Ten Note a medical app?

No. Ten Note is a general personal tracking tool and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
