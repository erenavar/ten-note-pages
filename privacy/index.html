Ten Note

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Ten Note is a personal scoring and list-tracking app published by Eren Avar in Türkiye. It does not require an account and does not send list content to an application server.

Information stored on your device

Ten Note stores list names, item text, scores and score history, score direction, language, and visual theme locally on your device.

The app contains no advertising, analytics, tracking, or crash-reporting SDK. It does not request access to your location, contacts, camera, or microphone.

Operating-system backups

On Android, Ten Note disables cloud backup and restore of its app files. On Android 12 and later, some device manufacturers may still transfer app files directly between devices. The Ten Note publisher does not receive or access either type of platform copy.

On iOS, an iPhone backup stored in iCloud or on your computer may include Ten Note's local database, depending on your Apple backup settings. These backups are managed through Apple's backup systems and your chosen storage. The publisher does not receive, access, or control their contents.

Backups and sharing

You may create an encrypted .tennote backup. The file is encrypted on your device using AES-256-GCM with a key derived from your password. Ten Note does not store or recover that password. Creating a new backup requires at least 10 characters. Backups created by earlier versions under the previous six-character minimum remain importable with their original password.

The manual backup file leaves the app only when you choose a destination in the system share sheet. A storage or messaging provider you select may process the file under its own privacy terms. Ten Note does not automatically upload backups.

When importing, Ten Note reads only the file you select through the system document picker. Imported files are limited to 10 MB. The temporary copy created in the app cache is deleted after processing, including when import fails or the file is rejected for size.

Retention and deletion

Local information remains until you delete content, use Settings → Delete all local data, or uninstall the app. Exported backups must be deleted separately from wherever you saved or shared them. iPhone backup copies stored in iCloud or on your computer are managed separately through your Apple backup settings and chosen storage.

Because Ten Note has no account or application server, the publisher does not hold a server-side copy that can be retrieved or deleted.

Security

Ten Note uses device-local storage and encrypted backups to reduce unnecessary exposure. No storage method is guaranteed to be completely secure. Protect your device, choose a strong backup password, and use services you trust.

Health disclaimer

Ten Note is a general personal tracking tool. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment and is not a substitute for professional care.

Website hosting

This policy covers the Ten Note mobile app. This page is hosted by GitHub Pages; GitHub may process technical website-access data under its own privacy terms. Opening this page from Ten Note does not add your list content to the request.

Changes and contact

The policy may be updated when the app or legal obligations change. The date above identifies the current version.
